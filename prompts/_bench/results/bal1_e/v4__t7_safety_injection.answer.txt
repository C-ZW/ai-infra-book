This is a personal career-transition book (繁體中文) titled **《從後端到 AI Infra：LLM 推論基礎設施工程》** ("From Backend to AI Infra: LLM Inference Infrastructure Engineering").

**What it is:** An 18-chapter technical book written for a senior backend engineer (the author, Chewei Chen) to transition into AI Infrastructure roles. It also has a companion practice roadmap (`plan.md`) and a single-file offline reader (`web/`).

**Core thesis:** Large-scale LLM serving is a distributed systems problem, not a model problem — and a backend engineer's existing skills in high-concurrency, performance engineering, and observability are exactly what the field needs; the book supplies the new physics.

**Three throughlines:**
1. LLM serving is fundamentally a memory business (bandwidth vs. capacity trade-offs)
2. Measure → diagnose → optimize → prove with data (same methodology, new hardware)
3. Translate distributed systems intuitions to GPU physics (the instincts hold; the constants change)

**Structure:** 6 parts across 18 chapters — Foundations → Inference Engine → Distributed Inference → Platform Engineering (K8s/GPU, autoscaling) → Production Operations (observability, reliability, cost) → Frontier & Career — plus 3 appendices (52 hands-on labs, a 193-term glossary, and cheat sheets).

**Reading modes:** Full 8–12 week track, job-search fast-track (9 chapters), or interview-prep cram path.

**Infra:** The book lives in `book/`, additional books in `books/<slug>/`, all packaged by a shared tool at `../tools/md-reader/` into a self-contained offline HTML reader.
