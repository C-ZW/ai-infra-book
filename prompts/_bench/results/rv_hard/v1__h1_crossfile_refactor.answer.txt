One judgment call worth flagging: I treated the **last** whitespace token as the surname, so `"Mary Jane Watson"` → `"Watson, Mary Jane"`. If your data instead has multi-word surnames (e.g. `"Ada Van Helsing"` meaning surname "Van Helsing"), this splits it the wrong way — there's no way to disambiguate `"first middle last"` from `"first last-part-1 last-part-2"` from a single flat string.
